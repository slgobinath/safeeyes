## The 20-20-20 Rule

The **20-20-20 rule** is the single most effective and widely recommended technique to reduce digital eye strain (also known as Computer Vision Syndrome). It was popularized by Dr. Jeffrey Anshel and is endorsed by the American Academy of Ophthalmology and the American Optometric Association.

### What Is the 20-20-20 Rule?

Every **20 minutes**, look at something **20 feet** (approximately 6 meters) away for at least **20 seconds**.

### Why It Works

- **Relaxes the ciliary muscle**: When you focus on a near object (like a screen), the ciliary muscle in your eye is contracted. Looking at a distant object allows this muscle to relax, reducing fatigue.
- **Reduces accommodative spasm**: Prolonged near-focus can cause the focusing mechanism to lock up, making it temporarily difficult to see distant objects clearly.
- **Promotes blink recovery**: During focused screen work, blink rates drop from ~15 blinks/minute to as low as 5-7 blinks/minute. The 20-second break encourages more natural blinking, redistributing the tear film.

### How to Implement It

1. **Use SafeEyes**: Configure SafeEyes to remind you every 20 minutes. Set the break duration to at least 20 seconds (longer is even better - 30-60 seconds provides more complete recovery).
2. **Pick a distant focal point**: Identify an object outside a window, a far wall, or a distant building before you start working.
3. **Focus actively, not passively**: Don't just glance - actually focus your eyes on the distant object for the full duration.
4. **Combine with stretching**: Use the 20-second window to also stretch your neck, shoulders, and wrists for a holistic micro-break.

### Advanced Variations

| Variation | Description | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **20-20-20+** | Extend the distant viewing to 60 seconds | Heavy screen users (8+ hrs/day) |
| **10-10-10** | Every 10 min, look 10 feet away for 10 sec | People with existing eye strain |
| **Every-Blink Method** | Consciously blink fully every 2-3 minutes | People with dry eye symptoms |

## Ergonomics & Workstation Setup

Poor ergonomics contribute significantly to eye strain, neck pain, and headaches - the classic triad of Computer Vision Syndrome.

### Monitor Placement

| Parameter | Recommendation | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **Distance** | 20-26 inches (50-65 cm) from eyes | Reduces accommodative demand |
| **Height** | Top of screen at or slightly below eye level | Natural downward gaze reduces ocular surface exposure |
| **Tilt** | Slight backward tilt (10-20 degrees) | Reduces neck strain |
| **Position** | Directly in front of you, centered | Reduces eye muscle fatigue |

### Dual Monitor Setup

- **Primary monitor**: Place directly in front of you.
- **Secondary monitor**: Same height and distance, angled about 30 degrees toward you.
- **Minimize head turning**: Position the dividing line at your nose.

### Lighting & Glare

1. **Position your screen perpendicular to windows**: Avoid direct backlight or reflections.
2. **Use window coverings**: Blinds, curtains, or anti-glare window films reduce daylight glare.
3. **Adjust ambient lighting**: Aim for 300-500 lux at your desk.
4. **Use an anti-glare screen filter**: Can reduce reflections by 85-95%.
5. **Task lighting**: Use a desk lamp with a diffuser for printed materials.

### Desk & Chair

- **Desk height**: Elbows at 90 degrees when typing; forearms parallel to the floor.
- **Chair**: Lumbar support; feet flat on the floor or on a footrest.
- **Document holder**: Position between keyboard and monitor at the same viewing distance.

## Screen Settings & Display Optimization

Your monitor settings have a direct impact on eye comfort.

### Brightness

- **Match screen brightness to surroundings**: The screen should be approximately the same brightness as your workspace.
- **Automatic adjustment**: Use Windows Night light, macOS True Tone, or f.lux/Redshift.
- **Avoid working in complete darkness**: High contrast causes eye strain.

### Color Temperature

| Time of Day | Recommended Temperature | Effect |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| Morning | 5500-6500K (neutral/cool) | Promotes alertness |
| Afternoon | 4500-5500K (neutral) | Balanced for extended work |
| Evening | 2700-3500K (warm) | Reduces blue light exposure |
| Night (after 9 PM) | 1900-2700K (very warm/amber) | Preserves circadian rhythm |

### Text Size & Contrast

- **Increase text size**: At least 14pt for body text; 125-150% browser zoom.
- **Use high-contrast themes**: Dark text on light background or vice versa.
- **Adjust ClearType/subpixel rendering**: Run ClearType Text Tuner (Windows) or enable Font smoothing (macOS).

### Refresh Rate & Flicker

- **Minimum 60 Hz refresh rate**: Higher rates (120/144 Hz) reduce imperceptible flicker.
- **Avoid PWM-dimming monitors**: Look for flicker-free or DC dimming technology.

### Monitor Type Considerations

- **IPS panels**: Better viewing angles and color consistency.
- **Matte vs. glossy**: Matte preferred for most office environments.
- **Screen resolution**: Higher resolution means sharper text at smaller sizes.

## Long-Term Eye Health Habits

Daily micro-habits compound into long-term eye health.

### Regular Eye Exams

- **Annual comprehensive eye exams**: Detect early signs of digital eye strain, glaucoma, macular degeneration.
- **Tell your optometrist about screen use**: They may prescribe computer glasses for the 20-26 inch distance.
- **Check for astigmatism**: Even mild astigmatism (0.5-0.75 diopters) can cause significant screen discomfort.

### Computer Glasses & Blue Light Filtering

- **Computer glasses**: Modified prescription optimized for intermediate screen distance.
- **Blue light filtering lenses**: Many users report improved comfort and better sleep with evening use.
- **Anti-reflective coating**: Essential for glasses wearers to reduce compounding reflections.

### Nutrition for Eye Health

| Nutrient | Benefit | Food Sources |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Lutein & Zeaxanthin** | Filters harmful blue light in the macula | Spinach, kale, broccoli, eggs |
| **Omega-3 Fatty Acids** | Supports tear production; reduces dry eye | Salmon, sardines, flaxseed, walnuts |
| **Vitamin A** | Essential for retinal function | Carrots, sweet potatoes, liver |
| **Vitamin C** | Reduces cataract risk; supports eye blood vessels | Citrus fruits, bell peppers, strawberries |
| **Vitamin E** | Protects eye cells from oxidative damage | Almonds, sunflower seeds, avocado |
| **Zinc** | Helps transport vitamin A to the retina | Oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds |

### Hydration & Dry Eye Prevention

- **Drink 8+ glasses of water daily**: Critical for tear production.
- **Use artificial tears**: Preservative-free drops, 4-6 times/day, proactively.
- **Avoid fans/AC blowing on your face**: Moving air accelerates tear evaporation.
- **Consider a humidifier**: Aim for 40-60% humidity in your workspace.
- **Warm compresses**: 5-10 minutes, 1-2 times daily, to unblock meibomian glands.

### Sleep & Circadian Rhythm

- **Screen curfew 1-2 hours before bed**: Blue light suppresses melatonin production.
- **7-9 hours of quality sleep**: Eyes recover and repair during sleep.
- **Dark bedroom**: Even small LED indicators can affect circadian rhythms.

### Exercise & Outdoor Time

- **2+ hours of outdoor time daily**: Reduces myopia risk; provides distance viewing.
- **Eye exercises**: Palming, figure-eight tracing, and focus-shifting provide temporary relief.
- **The 20-20-20 rule is an exercise**: It trains your focusing system to stay flexible.

## Quick Reference Checklist

Use this daily checklist to maintain your eye health:

- [ ] I followed the 20-20-20 rule today (SafeEyes helped!)
- [ ] My screen brightness matches my ambient lighting
- [ ] My monitor is 20-26 inches from my eyes
- [ ] The top of my screen is at or slightly below eye level
- [ ] I used artificial tears at least 2-3 times during screen work
- [ ] I drank at least 8 glasses of water today
- [ ] I spent some time outdoors looking at distant objects
- [ ] I adjusted my screen color temperature for the time of day
- [ ] I took at least one 5-10 minute break per hour away from all screens
- [ ] I will stop screen use at least 1 hour before bedtime tonight
