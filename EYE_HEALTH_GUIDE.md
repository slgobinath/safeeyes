# Digital Eye Health Guide

## 20-20-20 Rule
The 20‑20‑20 rule is a simple, effective method to reduce eye strain while using screens:
- Every **20 minutes**, look at something **20 feet** away.
- Keep your focus on the distant object for at least **20 seconds**.
- This gives the eye muscles a chance to relax and reduces fatigue.

## Ergonomics
Proper workstation ergonomics help keep your eyes comfortable and your posture healthy:
- **Screen distance**: Position the monitor about an arm’s length (20‑30 inches) from your eyes.
- **Screen height**: The top of the screen should be at or slightly below eye level.
- **Lighting**: Use ambient lighting to avoid glare; place the screen perpendicular to windows when possible.
- **Posture**: Sit upright with feet flat on the floor and use a chair that supports your lower back.
- **Blinking**: Remember to blink regularly to keep your eyes lubricated.

## Screen Settings
Adjusting display settings can significantly reduce strain:
- **Brightness**: Match the screen brightness to the surrounding environment.
- **Contrast**: Increase contrast for clearer text.
- **Color temperature**: Use a warmer colour temperature (around 4000 K) in the evening.
- **Font size**: Choose a readable font size; increase scaling if needed.
- **Blue‑light filter**: Enable a built‑in blue‑light reduction mode or use third‑party software such as f.lux.

## Long‑Term Habits
Building sustainable habits protects your vision over the years:
- **Regular eye exams**: Schedule comprehensive eye examinations at least once every two years.
- **Hydration**: Stay hydrated to keep your eyes moist.
- **Breaks**: Take short breaks not only for your eyes but also to stretch and move.
- **Screen‑time balance**: Alternate screen‑intensive tasks with non‑screen activities.
- **Nutrition**: Eat foods rich in omega‑3 fatty acids, lutein, and vitamin A which support eye health.

Integrating these practices with Safe Eyes reminders helps maintain healthy vision while using digital devices.
